Take Action




Tip 1

It’s tempting to buy cheap clothes, but low prices often mean low quality. Instead of frequently replacing worn-out items, choose better-quality pieces that last longer and avoid brands known for their fast fashion practices. This not only helps reduce waste but also saves you money in the long run. By selecting items built to last, you reduce your environmental footprint. Click the button below to learn more about fast fashion brands you should avoid.

Tip 2

More fashion companies are focusing on ethical and eco-friendly practices, offering you the chance to support a cleaner industry. Yes, it may be easier to grab something from fast fashion brands, but voting with your wallet by choosing sustainable labels can help shift the fashion industry towards better practices. With time, these options are becoming more affordable and available. Click the button below to find some brands available in your area.

Tipe 3

Before you decide to throw away old clothes, consider alternatives. Many fabrics, especially synthetics, don't decompose quickly in landfills. Instead, try repairing damaged clothing or alter it to fit better, donating your unused items to someone who might need them, or sell gently used items through thrift stores or apps. Click the button below to learn more about how you can reuse and recycle old clothes.

Tip 4

Every piece of clothing you purchase has an environmental cost, from production to transport. The issue isn't just how clothes are made, but how much we buy. Our wardrobes have grown larger over time, even though we really don’t need as much. Instead of buying on impulse, ask yourself if you can make do with what you already own or if the item will serve you long-term.

Tip 5

Buying second-hand is a great way to find unique pieces and reduce waste. Thrift stores, online platforms, and vintage shops are full of hidden gems. You can also organize a clothing swap with friends or participate in community swaps, giving your wardrobe a refresh without buying new. Renting clothes for special events is another option, helping you stay fashionable without the waste.

Tip 6

Washing and drying clothes can use a lot of water and energy. By washing at lower temperatures, skipping the dryer, and only laundering when necessary, you can significantly reduce your impact. Simple changes like air-drying and using eco-friendly detergents can also protect your clothes and the environment.s
