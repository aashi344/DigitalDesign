Unsustainable Cities

An unsustainable city is an urban area that grows rapidly without planning for the long-term needs of its residents or the environment. These cities consume resources recklessly, produce excessive waste, and rely heavily on fossil fuels, which is why they experience higher levels of pollution, overcrowding, and environmental degradation. As a result, individuals residing in unsustainable cities face health issues and long term difficulties. Unsustainable cities prioritize short-term gains over long-term well-being, creating harmful living conditions for their inhabitants.

Health Impacts of Sustainable Cities
- High levels of air pollution from industries and vehicles lead to respiratory problems, heart disease, and cancer.
- Overcrowding and inadequate sanitation systems spread diseases like cholera, typhoid, and other infections.
- Lack of green spaces leaves residents with fewer opportunities for exercise and relaxation, contributing to obesity and poor mental well-being.
- Poor waste management results in unhygienic living conditions, attracting pests and causing diseases.

Sustainable Cities

A sustainable city is an urban area designed to meet the needs of its residents wihtout compromising the ability of future generations to thrive. These cities are designed with the well-being of their residents and the environment in mind, focusing on reducing pollution, conserving resources, and creating spaces that promote a better quality of life for everyone. The result is a healthier, more inclusive urban environment where resources are used responsibly, and everyone can benefit.

Health Impacts of Sustainable Cities
- Cleaner air due to reduced reliance on fossil fuels improves respiratory health and lowers the risk of lung diseases.
- Access to safe drinking water and proper sanitation prevents the spread of waterborne diseases.
- Green spaces like parks and urban forests reduce stress, encourage physical activity, and improve mental well-being.
- Reduced traffic congestion and noise pollution create a calmer, more livable environment for all residents
